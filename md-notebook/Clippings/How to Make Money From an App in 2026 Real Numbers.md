Most guides on app monetization focus on the models. The missing piece is the math. Here's how the numbers actually look for indie apps in 2026.

## The Six Models, Ranked by Indie-Fit

| Model | Best for | Realistic Revenue | Effort |
|-------|----------|-------------------|--------|
| Subscriptions | Tools used weekly+ | $3–15/user/mo × 2–5% conversion | Medium |
| Freemium + one-time unlock | Single-job utilities | $5–30 once × 2–8% conversion | Low-Medium |
| Consumable IAP | AI features, credits | Varies (price above inference cost) | Medium |
| Ads | High-volume, casual apps | ~$5–20 RPM | Low |
| Paid download | Almost no one in 2026 | Suppresses installs 10× | Low |
| B2B licensing | Niche pro tools | $20–200/seat/month | High (sales) |

## Subscriptions: The Default for a Reason

The math for a niche utility app:

> 1,000 downloads/month × 8% trial start × 40% trial-to-paid × $6/month ≈ $190/month of new recurring revenue

That $190 compounds. Month 12: $2,280/month if you hold those subscribers. The key variable isn't price — it's trial-to-paid conversion, which comes down entirely to how good the first 5 minutes feel.

**Critical rules:**
- Annual plan should be the default selection
- Gate repeat-use value, not first-run experience
- Paywall after the user's first success moment, not before

## The Annual Plan Advantage

Annual subscribers generate 2.3× more lifetime revenue than monthly-only subscribers. Churn in months 1–3 is 8× lower.

The mental model: monthly subscribers try your app. Annual subscribers committed to your app.

How to lead with annual without feeling pushy:
- Show annual first (left or top)
- Display the per-month equivalent: "$4.17/month, billed annually"
- Mark it "Best Value" or "Most Popular"

## Freemium + One-Time Unlock

"Pay $12 once, unlock everything." Clean, simple, no recurring charge anxiety. But: you only get paid once per user, and value doesn't compound. Right for: unit converters, calculators, single-purpose tools. Wrong for: habit-forming apps where subscription potential is real.

## Consumable IAP for AI Apps

If each generation costs you $0.03 in API calls, charge $0.10–0.15 per generation. Price credits above inference cost with enough margin to absorb 15–30% app store commission.

Watch out: Apple takes its cut from every purchase, including consumables.

## Ads: The Math is Usually Bad

At $10 RPM (per 1,000 impressions), you need 100,000 monthly sessions to make $1,000. Without a realistic path to six-figure session counts, ads distract and frustrate without meaningful revenue.

Ads work for casual games and daily-use utilities with massive scale. They don't work for niche tools with 1,000–10,000 MAU.

## B2B Licensing: The Overlooked Model

Selling to businesses, not individuals:
- 20 gyms at $50/month = $1,000 MRR
- Vs. 1,000 consumers at 2% conversion to a $5/month plan = $100 MRR

Businesses have higher willingness to pay, lower churn, and post-2024 rules allow web-based billing for business accounts — often bypassing app store commission entirely.

## Picking Your Model (60-Second Framework)

- Used daily/weekly, accumulates user data → **subscription**
- Single task, occasional use → **one-time unlock**
- Each use costs you money (API) → **credits**
- Massive casual audience → **ads (rewarded video)**
- Users are businesses → **B2B seats**

## The Uncomfortable Truth

"Monetization model is the smallest variable. Revenue = audience × value × model, in that order."

A mediocre paywall on a beloved app outperforms the perfect paywall on an app nobody uses. The 2026 advantage: building costs dropped to near-zero, so you can empirically find audience-value fit before investing heavily in monetization optimization.

Ship, track retention, then add the paywall.
