RevenueCat's annual State of Subscription Apps report draws on data from over 30,000 apps and hundreds of millions of subscribers. The 2026 edition surfaces clear patterns for what makes subscription businesses grow.

## The Benchmark Numbers That Matter

- **Median trial-to-paid conversion**: 26% (up from 23% in 2025)
- **Day 1 trial cancellation rate**: 47% — nearly half of users cancel before the trial ends
- **Annual plan revenue share**: Apps with annual plans generate 2.3x more revenue per subscriber than monthly-only apps
- **Median monthly revenue for top quartile indie apps**: $4,200/month

## The Annual Plan Advantage

The single highest-leverage pricing decision: offering an annual plan. Apps that default to showing annual pricing first convert better and retain longer. The math is simple — annual subscribers churn 8x less than monthly subscribers in the first 90 days.

Best practices for annual pricing:

- Display annual as the default (monthly as the "other option")
- Anchor the monthly equivalent ("just $4.99/month, billed annually")
- Offer a short trial on annual too — it doesn't hurt conversion

## Where Subscribers Drop Off

The data shows a predictable drop-off curve:

1. **Download → trial start**: Lost 60–80% (many downloads never start a trial)
2. **Trial start → trial end**: Lost another 47% (Day 1 cancellations dominate)
3. **Trial end → paid**: Convert ~26% of remaining
4. **Month 1 → Month 3**: Lose another 30% of paid subscribers

The highest-ROI intervention is improving the trial experience during the first 24 hours. Users who hit a core "aha moment" in the first session retain at 3x the rate.

## AI Features and Monetization

Apps with AI features show notably higher willingness to pay. In the 2026 data:

- AI-enhanced apps see 31% higher ARPU (average revenue per user) than non-AI equivalents
- Users cite "saves me time" as the top reason for subscribing to AI-powered tools
- Credit-based AI features (pay per generation) outperform unlimited plans for high-cost inference

## The Paywall Placement Finding

Counter-intuitively, apps that show the paywall later in onboarding — after users have experienced value — convert better than apps that paywall at first launch. The sweet spot is after the user's first meaningful action, not before.

## Platform Split

- iOS users generate 2.1x the revenue of Android users at equivalent install volumes
- Android's share of new subscriber growth is rising (up 8 points year-over-year), driven by emerging markets
- Web subscriptions (sold via Stripe, bypassing app store fees) are growing fast for tools with a web component — up 34% YoY

## What Separates Top-Quartile Apps

Apps in the top revenue quartile share common traits:

1. Annual plan is prominently featured
2. Onboarding is personalized to the user's stated goal
3. Trial experience delivers a clear win within 60 seconds
4. Push notifications are used judiciously (2–3/week max) for re-engagement
5. Paused subscriptions are offered before cancellation
